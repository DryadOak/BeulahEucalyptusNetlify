FAQ

Do you offer delivery?
Generally, we sell our foliage on a local person to person basis but we aim to make our service accessible for everyone, so please do get in touch and we'll see what we can arrange. Delivery is available for all our oil products.
How do I care for my foliage and wreaths?
Store your fresh foliage out of direct sunlight in a cool place with the bottom of the stems in water. Let us know if you need foliage for hot marquees, dried bunches or other special circumstances. Water misting is great for your wreaths; Eucalyptus thrives in a slightly humid environment. Regularly mist the foliage or wreath with water to provide moisture and prevent drying out. Avoid over-saturating, as excessive moisture can lead to mould or decay.
Can I purchase eucalyptus foliage in the summer month?
Eucalyptus is primarily a winter foliage and as the weather warms it can look a little different then you expect. Whilst the young leaves develop for the cooler season, we can offer adult foliage, often with willow and almond shaped leaves. There are also flowers, buds and seeded stems available and we can let you know what flora are looking best for mixed bunches.
Why do you sell foliage by the stem?
Foliage is sold by the stem because it gives you a better idea of the value you get for your money. Many factors like stem thickness and leaf size creates too much variation in weight.
How can I dry my eucalyptus?
Hang the foliage bunches upside down in a cool, dark, and well-ventilated area. This drying method helps to retain the shape of the eucalyptus and will usually take around two to three weeks. The colour may fade and leaves become fragile, so we recommend you display them somewhere they are unlikely to get knocked.

Fun Facts

  • Eucalyptus is a diverse genus, comprising over 700 species. It is known for its impressive variety of forms, including tall trees, mallee shrubs, and even eucalyptus species that resemble small bushes
  • Many eucalyptus species have adaptations that help them survive and even benefit from wildfires. They have thick, fibrous bark that can withstand intense heat, and some species produce seeds that germinate after a fire, taking advantage of the cleared landscape.
  • Eucalyptus trees are known for their fast growth rates. Some species can grow several feet per year, making them one of the fastest-growing tree genera.
  • Indigenous Australians have long used eucalyptus leaves for various medicinal purposes. They would crush the leaves and use them as poultices for treating wounds, respiratory ailments, and other conditions.
